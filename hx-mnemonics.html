🔬 The HPI — how the pain behaves

LOPQRST
The “How” of the HPI — SOAP_Notes.pdf
  • L Location — where is it?
  • O Onset — when/how did it start?
  • P Prior / Palliative / Provocative / Progression — the four P's (has it happened before, what helps, what makes it worse, is it better/worse/same?)
  • Q Quality — sharp, dull, achy, burning?
  • R Radiation — does it travel?
  • S Severity — 0–10 scale
  • T Timing — constant, intermittent, time of day?
Memory hookRead it as “LOP-QRST” — the “QRST” tail is the same run of letters as an EKG wave (Q-R-S-T), so if you can trace a heartbeat you can trace a pain history. The lone P is the multitasker — picture a “P for Plenty,” because it alone carries all four P's (Prior, Palliative, Provocative, Progression).

📋 Past Medical History

FAOMASH
Past Medical History — PastMedicalHistory.pdf
  • F Family history
  • A Allergies
  • O Other illness — chronic conditions / other doctors seen
  • M Medications
  • A Accidents
  • S Surgeries
  • H Hospitalizations
Memory hookSay it out loud — “FAH-oh-mash” — like mashing the patient's whole past into one word. Watch the two A's: the first is Allergies (near the front, the thing you confirm every visit), the second is Accidents (later, tucked between Meds and Surgeries).
✅ FAOMASH note. On the actual PMH slide the letters are printed spaced out as F A O M A S H — so if you search (Ctrl-F / grep) the deck for the word “FAOMASH,” you get zero hits and think it isn't there. It is. Look for the spaced letters, not the closed-up word.

🧡 Review of Systems — the 19 systems

The 19 systems of the ROS
ReviewOfSystems.pdf — screen head-to-toe so nothing is missed
1. Constitutional / General 2. Skin / Integumentary 3. Head 4. Eyes 5. Ears 6. Nose & Sinuses 7. Mouth & Throat 8. Neck 9. Cardiovascular 10. Respiratory 11. GI 12. GU (Urinary + Genital) 13. Musculoskeletal 14. Peripheral Vascular 15. Neurologic 16. Psychiatric 17. Endocrine 18. Hematologic / Lymphatic
Count check: the deck lists 19 systems. The 18 pills above are the distinct headings; the 19th falls out when you split GU into its two parts — Urinary and Genital — which the slide names together but screens separately.
Memory hookWalk it top-to-bottom like getting dressed for the day, then check the wiring: start at the outside and the head (General, Skin, then Head→Eyes→Ears→Nose→Mouth→Neck), drop into the chest and belly (Cardio, Respiratory, GI, GU), swing out to the frame and limbs (Musculoskeletal, Peripheral Vascular), then finish with the “control systems” — Neuro, Psych, Endocrine, Heme/Lymph. Nose-to-toes, then the wiring.
ABCDE
The skin/lesion check inside the ROS — ReviewOfSystems.pdf
  • A Asymmetry
  • B Border
  • C Color
  • D Diameter
  • E Evolving
Memory hookIt's literally the first five letters of the alphabet — a mole that is “out of order” on any of the ABC's is the one to worry about. E for Evolving is the one people forget: a spot that changes over time is the biggest red flag.

👨‍❤️‍👨 Sexual history

CDC — the 5 P's
Sexual history — CDC framework
  • P Partners
  • P Practices
  • P Protection for STDs
  • P Past history of STDs
  • P Prevention of pregnancy
Memory hookFive P's, and they run in a natural story order: who (Partners), what (Practices), how you stay safe (Protection), what's happened before (Past STDs), and babies (Prevention of pregnancy). Who → what → safety → history → pregnancy.
Fenway — the 8 P's
Sexual history, expanded — Fenway framework
  • P Preferences
  • P Partners
  • P Practices
  • P Protection for STIs
  • P Past history of STIs
  • P Pregnancy
  • P Pleasure
  • P Partner Violence
Memory hookFenway takes the CDC 5 and adds 3 more P's on top — Preferences, Pleasure, Partner Violence — the three that make it patient-centered rather than just infection-focused. Remember it as “the CDC 5, plus the 3 the CDC left out”: it opens with Preferences (identity first) and closes with Pleasure & Partner Violence (wellbeing & safety).

🤱 Obstetric history

GPA
Obstetric shorthand — e.g. G4P3A1
  • G Gravida — number of pregnancies
  • P Para — number of births
  • A Abortus — number of losses
Memory hookG→P→A tracks a pregnancy from start to outcome: Gravida is every time she got pregnant, Para is every pregnancy that was produced (a birth), Abortus is the ones absent (losses). In G4P3A1, the 3 births + 1 loss add up to the 4 pregnancies.

📝 The note & the interview structure

SOAP
The 4 sections of the note — know what goes in each
  • S Subjective — what the patient tells you: chief complaint, HPI / history
  • O Objective — what you measure: vitals, physical exam, labs/data
  • A Assessment — diagnoses & differential
  • P Plan — the action plan
Memory hookThe split that's tested most is S vs O: Subjective = what they SAY (history/HPI — you take it on their word), Objective = what you can OBSERVE or measure (vitals, exam — a machine or your hands could confirm it). If a number or finding would still be true with the patient silent, it's Objective.
The 3 Functions of the Medical Interview
Cole & Bird — HxTaking_Intro.pdf
  • 1 Build the relationship
  • 2 Assess / understand the problem
  • 3 Manage / collaborate on the plan
Memory hookThink “Connect → Collect → Collaborate” (three C's for the three functions). You can't collect a good story until you connect, and you can't collaborate on a plan until you've collected the problem — they run in order.

💕 Empathy & communication models

Empathy vs Sympathy vs Compassion
Three often-confused terms — HxTaking_Intro / Communication_Empathy
  • E Empathy — understand and share another's feelings; put yourself in their shoes
  • S Sympathy — feeling for someone (pity/sharing feelings from the outside)
  • C Compassion — awareness of distress plus a desire to alleviate it (adds action)
Memory hookLadder of closeness and action: Sympathy feels for you from across the room, Empathy feels with you (in your shoes), and Compassion adds the hand that helps you up — it's the only one with a “do something about it” built in.
Carl Rogers' 3 Qualities
The therapeutic qualities of the practitioner
  • 1 Congruence / genuineness
  • 2 Unconditional positive regard
  • 3 Empathic understanding
Memory hookRogers wants you to be Real, Warm, and Understanding — genuine (no fake bedside act), accepting no matter what they disclose (unconditional positive regard), and tuned in to what they feel (empathic understanding). Real → Warm → Tuned-in.
Coulehan's Levels of Empathic Response
Communication_Empathy.pdf — two helpful, two harmful
  • + Interchangeable — matches the patient's expressed feeling (positive)
  • + Additive — goes one step deeper than what was said (positive)
  • − Ignoring — skips the feeling entirely (negative)
  • − Minimizing — responds at a lesser level than the concern (negative)
Memory hookLine them up as a thermometer against the patient's feeling: Additive is above, Interchangeable is right at it (both good), while Minimizing lands below and Ignoring is off the scale (both bad). The two that start with vowels-ish “good” energy meet the patient; the two that start with I/M fall short.
Cultural Competence vs Cultural Humility
Bates vs Coulehan framing — CulturalCompetence_Humility.pdf
  • C Competence — knowledge & skills; risks becoming a “list of do's and don'ts”
  • H Humility — lifelong self-reflection & self-critique, and addressing the power imbalance
Memory hookCompetence sounds like a finish line (“I'm certified, I know this culture”), Humility is a road with no end (“the more I learn, the more I realize I don't know”). Competence = a closed checklist; Humility = an open mirror you keep looking into. If the answer implies “done”, it's Competence; if it implies “ongoing”, it's Humility.

📚 The number that anchors the whole course

80–90%
The share of the diagnosis that comes from the history
Roughly 80–90% of the diagnosis comes from the history — before a single lab or exam maneuver. It's the reason this entire course exists.
Memory hookThink “the history is an A-minus of the whole diagnosis” — roughly 8 or 9 out of every 10 answers are already in the patient's story if you take it well. Exam and labs are just confirming the last 10–20%.
