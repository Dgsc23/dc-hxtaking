Medical Abbreviations — Exam Cheat Sheet

CBS0201 History Taking · the professor’s medical-abbreviation handout, in his exact order
Cumulative-tested — abbreviations are double-weighted on the written final. Reproduced verbatim; nothing added.

⚠️ Watch out for these

  • QD (“every day”) and QOD (“every other day”) — WRITE THESE OUT to avoid confusion. They are on the JCAHO forbidden list.
  • L / R are often circled on the handout — that circle is the abbreviation for Left / Right.
  • The symbols trip people up: c = with, s = without, p = after (each is the letter with an overbar), plus Δ = change and Ø = none.
  • GPA is read in G·P·A order: G4P3A1 = 4 pregnancies, 3 births, 1 abortion/miscarriage. Note the “No abbreviation” cases — multigravida and multipara are written out.
